How to Convert PSI to Bar

Converting PSI to Bar is simple. The conversion factor is 1 PSI = 0.0689476 Bar. To convert any PSI value to Bar, multiply the pressure in PSI by 0.0689476. For example, to convert 35 PSI to Bar, the calculation is 35 × 0.0689476 = 2.4132 Bar.

The reverse conversion from Bar to PSI uses the factor 1 Bar = 14.5038 PSI. Multiply any Bar value by 14.5038 to get the equivalent pressure in PSI. Our converter handles both directions with a single click of the swap button.

The PSI to Bar Conversion Formula

The exact mathematical formula to convert PSI to Bar is:

Bar = PSI × 0.0689476

And to convert Bar to PSI:

PSI = Bar × 14.5038

These conversion factors are derived from the definition of the two pressure units. One pound per square inch (PSI) equals 6894.76 Pascals, while one Bar equals 100,000 Pascals. Dividing 6894.76 by 100,000 gives the conversion factor 0.0689476.

Tire Pressure: PSI vs Bar Explained

In the United States, tire pressure is measured in PSI (pounds per square inch). Most US vehicles have a sticker on the driver's door jamb showing the recommended tire pressure in PSI, typically between 30 and 36 PSI for passenger cars.

In Europe, Asia and most other regions, tire pressure is measured in Bar. If you drive a European vehicle or use a European tire pressure gauge, you will see Bar values instead of PSI. Common passenger car tire pressures range from 2.0 Bar to 2.5 Bar.

Knowing how to convert PSI to Bar is essential when traveling internationally, using imported vehicles, or working with tire equipment that displays a different unit than what you are accustomed to.

HVAC and AC Technicians

Scuba diving equipment uses both PSI and Bar for tank pressure readings. US-made regulators and gauges typically display PSI, while European equipment shows Bar.
